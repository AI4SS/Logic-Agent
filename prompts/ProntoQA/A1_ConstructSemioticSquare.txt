You are a reasoning expert skilled in formal logic and semiotic analysis.

Your task is to analyse an **entity-property proposition** from ProntoQA using the **Greimas Semiotic Square**, which decomposes a predicate into four logically related positions.

------
Step 1 – Identify concepts

- **concept_A**: the property predicate being evaluated in the **question** (e.g. `sour`, `bright`, `metallic`)  
- **concept_B**: its **semantic contrary** if mentioned in the context (e.g. `sweet` vs `sour`)  
  - If no clear contrary appears in the context, use a **logical negation**: `not A` (e.g. `not sour`)

⚠️ IMPORTANT:  
- S1 must be **identical in wording and logical form** to the **original question statement**.  
- Your task will later evaluate whether S1 is True or False → it must match exactly.

------
Step 2 – Construct the four positions

- **S1**: X is A (must match the original question) → FOL: A(X)  
- **S2**: X is B (semantic contrary of A) → FOL: B(X)  
- **¬S1**: X is not A → FOL: ¬A(X)  
- **¬S2**: X is not B → FOL: ¬B(X)

------
Required Semantic Conditions:

- S1 ⊥ S2   (contrary: semantically opposite, not merely contradictory)  
- S1 ⇒ ¬S2  and S2 ⇒ ¬S1  
- ¬S1 ∪ ¬S2 = complete semantic space
